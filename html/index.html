SLEEP IS VERY IMPORTANT

Welcome to Avita!

Sleep is one of the foundations of good health. This calculator will ask you to follow the popups, enter your age, and enter 7 sleep values separated by commas. After that, it will show your average sleep, recommended sleep, weekly sleep difference, a chart, and a recommendation.

Sleep Chart

Green bars meet or pass the recommendation, red bars are below it, the orange line is your average, and the blue dashed line is your recommended sleep.

The 4 Pillars of Sleep

1. Duration

Most adults need between 7-9 hours of sleep each night to be at their healthiest. Sleep debt is what happens when these sleep needs are not being met. For example,

if you need eight hours of sleep per night to feel well rested, and you only get six hours of sleep for two nights, you are two hours short each night and have built up four hours of sleep debt.

2. Continuity

Continuity is the ability to stay asleep without frequent wake-ups. Strong continuity helps prevent the tired feeling that can happen even after a long night in bed.

To improve this, keep your room cool, dark, and quiet, and avoid drinking large amounts of liquid right before bed.

3. Regularity

Regularity anchors your internal clock, making it easier to fall asleep and wake up naturally.

The best way to build regularity is to keep the same wake-up time every day, including weekends.

4. Depth

Sleep depth matters because deep sleep and REM sleep help with physical repair, immune function, memory, and emotional processing.

To improve this, limit alcohol and caffeine in the evening so your body can reach more restorative sleep stages.
