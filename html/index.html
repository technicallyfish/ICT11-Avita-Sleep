SLEEP IS VERY IMPORTANT

Welcome, , to the Avita Sleep Calculator!

Sleep, something that most of us take for granted, is a fundamental cornerstone for our health. Improving your sleep has compounding positive impacts not just on tomorrow, but on the rest of your life as well.

How to use this calculator:
Follow the popups as they appear on screen. You will be asked to enter your name and age , then input 7 sleep values separated by commas (one for each night of the past week, e.g. 7, 6.5, 8, 7, 5, 9, 6 ).

The program will then calculate your average sleep, your recommended sleep based on your age, your weekly sleep difference , display a chart of your sleep, and give you a personalized recommendation .

The 4 Pillars of Sleep

1. Duration

Most adults need between 7-9 hours of sleep each night to be at their healthiest. Sleep debt is what happens when these sleep needs aren't being met. For example,

if you need eight hours of sleep per night to feel well rested, and you've only gotten six hours of sleep for the past two nights; you'd be two hours short each night, and thus have accumulated four hours of sleep debt over those two days.

2. Continuity

Continuity is the ability to stay asleep without frequent wake-ups, which prevents the "fragmentation" that leaves you feeling groggy despite a long duration.

To improve this, optimize your environment by keeping your room cool, dark, and quiet, and avoid drinking large amounts of liquid right before bed to minimize middle-of-the-night disruptions.

3. Regularity

Regularity anchors your internal biological clock (circadian rhythm), making it easier to fall asleep and wake up naturally without an alarm.

The best way to use this is to stick to the same wake-up time every single day even on weekends which helps regulate the hormones that tell your body when to be alert and when to rest.

4. Depth

The value of depth lies in reaching the "slow-wave" and REM stages, which are critical for physical repair, immune function, and emotional processing.

To improve this, focus on limiting alcohol and caffeine in the evening, as these substances chemically block your brain from entering the most restorative phases of sleep.
